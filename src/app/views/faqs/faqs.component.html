Q What happens if I buy an NFT but then decide I don’t want to own an NFT lease but would rather just own the title?
A An NFT holder can elect to redeem their NFT at any point during the lease term with Zyfty. This will cause the NFT to be destroyed. See white paper and for details.
Q Can I look at the actual home representing the NFT before I enter into the purchase process (aka before I bid)?
A Most likely, yes. However, much like the traditional sales process, the extent to which viewings will be offered is up to the seller of the NFT.
Q Is there an escrow process upon an offer being accepted to purchase an NFT?
A Yes, there is an escrow process that is very similar to the current marketplace. Upon an offer, or bid for the home, a small amount of currency will be held in escrow as a “soft” deposit. Upon acceptance of an offer by the seller, an additional amount will be added to the “soft” deposit along with a “hard” deposit later in the escrow period. Like all real estate transactions, the length of the escrow period as well as “soft” and “hard” deposit amounts vary based on the NFT seller’s purchasing criteria. However, know that the NFT can transfer in less than a day.
Q How do I receive information regarding the home?
A In addition to the information noted in the NFT home listing on the Zyfty Marketplace, potential NFT buyers may purchase a “manifest” which includes all documents necessary to perform due diligence on the property represented by the NFT.
Q How do I pay my taxes and any fees associated with my home?
A The NFT includes mechanisms to property taxes, HOA fees and other regularly recurring home ownership costs.
Q Can I make improvements and upgrades to my home?
A An NFT holder can make improvements to the home and engage in other activities with the home just as in standard ownership.
Q What type of currency do you accept for an NFT purchase?
A Accepted currencies for purchase and all other payments associated with the NFT are USDC and ETH.
Q What type of token base is used for a Zyfty NFT?
A Zyfty created its own token base on the Ethereum network.
